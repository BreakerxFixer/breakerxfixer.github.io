We are BreakerxFixer, a small team focused on both offensive and defensive security, CTF challenges, and hands-on vulnerability research.
Our work revolves around breaking systems, hardening them, understanding how they fail, protecting them, and documenting the process through detailed writeups and Proofs of Concept.
We take a practical approach to cybersecurity — no empty theory, no recycled content — everything we share is built, tested, and understood from the ground up.
From exploiting vulnerabilities to analyzing defensive mechanisms, our goal is to cover the full attack surface and develop a deeper understanding of real-world security scenarios.
We are constantly learning, building, and refining our skills, aiming to contribute meaningful insights to the community while evolving as security researchers.

Kyrox

I’m Kyrox, a cybersecurity enthusiast who started exploring hacking in 2024 through online content, initially experimenting with Wi-Fi security and basic attack techniques. What began as curiosity quickly turned into a clear career path.
During my studies, I developed a strong foundation in Linux, while also learning multiple programming languages including Python, Bash, Java, and Rust. These skills allowed me to better understand how systems work — and how they can be broken.
While completing an internship, I built a fully functional website from scratch using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. That project became my first real testing ground for security, where I identified and explored several vulnerabilities, reinforcing my interest in web security.
I am currently pursuing a higher degree in Systems Administration (ASIR), where I’ve focused heavily on cybersecurity — especially offensive security and web hacking, which I’m most passionate about.
I consistently challenge myself by solving Hack The Box exercises on a daily basis and working towards certifications to validate and expand my knowledge.
My goal is to continuously improve, break systems to understand them, and evolve into a skilled security professional.

Winter

I’m 0xWinter, a freelance systems administrator with over 6 years of experience and a former sound technician — a background that shaped my attention to detail, precision, and problem-solving mindset.
I started working as an IT emergency technician, providing rapid-response support and repair services for small and medium-sized businesses, as well as hardware and mobile device repairs for individual clients. This hands-on experience built a strong technical foundation and the ability to operate under pressure.
Over time, my focus shifted towards cybersecurity through CTF competitions and structured learning. After achieving a 97% score in the eJPT certification, I transitioned into a defensive security path, specializing in blue team operations, digital forensics, and OSINT.
I have participated in countless CTF challenges, reaching the #1 position in Spain on TryHackMe for several consecutive months, demonstrating both consistency and depth in practical skills.
Additionally, I obtained the Google Certificate in Defensive Cybersecurity, further strengthening my expertise in threat detection, analysis, and response.
My approach is simple: understand systems deeply, identify their weaknesses, and use that knowledge to defend and improve them.
